Addiction Self-Assessment Questionnaire

This questionnaire consists of 20 yes/no questions designed to help you reflect on potential patterns of addictive behavior related to substances or activities like gambling, social media, alcohol, and drugs. Please answer honestly for the most accurate self-assessment.

Disclaimer: This questionnaire is for informational purposes only and is not a diagnostic tool. If you're concerned about addiction issues, please consult with a healthcare professional or addiction specialist.

Question 1: Do you find yourself spending more time than intended on your chosen activity or substance?

Question 2: Have you ever tried to cut down or stop but found it difficult?

Question 3: Do you feel restless, irritable, or anxious when unable to engage in your activity or use your substance of choice?

Question 4: Has your use of substances or participation in activities interfered with work, school, or family responsibilities?

Question 5: Have you ever lied to family or friends about how much you use or participate in your activity?

Question 6: Do you find yourself needing more of the substance or activity to get the same effect or satisfaction?

Question 7: Have you continued despite knowing it's causing problems in your relationships?

Question 8: Do you check social media within 5 minutes of waking up?

Question 9: Have you ever gambled more money than you could afford to lose?

Question 10: Do you drink alcohol to cope with stress or negative emotions?

Question 11: Have you ever neglected important social, occupational, or recreational activities because of substance use or other addictive behavior?

Question 12: Have you ever used substances or engaged in addictive behavior in situations where it was physically hazardous?

Question 13: Do you feel anxious when your phone battery is low or you're without internet access?

Question 14: Have friends or family expressed concern about your substance use or behavior?

Question 15: Do you find yourself thinking about your substance or activity even when you're not engaging in it?

Question 16: Have you ever experienced financial problems due to your substance use or activity?

Question 17: Do you feel guilty about your level of substance use or participation in addictive activities?

Question 18: Have you experienced physical health problems related to your substance use or activity but continued anyway?

Question 19: Do you use substances or engage in activities to avoid withdrawal symptoms?

Question 20: Have you made unsuccessful efforts to control, cut back, or stop your substance use or activity?
